Piracy and Culture

Pirates belong to a culture of freedom, tropics, and sailing. Space-pirates to a subculture of science and adventure. Many works, which may not explicitly be piratey, belong to these cultures. For example, not only piratey works but also tropical-country, sea shanties, and even space-westerns. Such works representing the culture have been listed here.

In addition to lists of source media there are a number of quotes pirates might appreciate. Preceding the page's end a justification for the romanization of pirates is given. Finally, there's an explanation of pirate's mentality at the bottom of the page.

Pirate's Mentality

Technically, a pirate is a violent-criminal of the sea; however, their romanization represents a particular kind of freedom. Where all freedom implies some sense of independence and choice, a pirate's freedom expresses loyalty to oneself. They make their own destiny, be their own persons, and take their own actions – often in defiance of their circumstances. Pirates take pride in owning their lives and show no shame in their will to better them. They have the conviction and will to make, own, and pursue their choices. Pirates are greedy.

A pirate's prerogative is the natural prerogative of every members of the organizational level Organism: to exist as a free individual. Undermining this prerogative, centralized collectivization re-embodies individuality unto itself. Such re-embodiment is the natural prerogative of a centralized collective, but pirates defy it. Defiance is a pirate's prerogative. In their view, it's their prerogative as free individuals. Their justification and defence is merely their pirate status.
