Mini-page: Intellectual Property Flaws

Can one own ideas?

Our society has made ideas* ownable, and equated IP-infringement to theft, but the system doesn't really work. Ideas have no clear boundary or origin. So, it's not that one can't lay or enforce claims. It's that one can't do so consistently.

Legal Trolls

Legal Trolls use suing or the threat of suing to make a profit. This is not a problem isolated to intellectual property. However, the cost of defence is excessive, and this discourages entrepreneurship.

Monopolies

Intellectual-property grants the exclusive right to reproduce and (consequently) to profit from a work to authors. This is a legal monopoly, which not only discourages but eliminates competition for a work.
