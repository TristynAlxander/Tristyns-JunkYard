Parody Status

This site is a parody of anti-piracy organizations. Namely, this site is a mockery of NoPiracy.org. Anti-piracy organizations have consistently gone to extremes, and as such so must this site. Consequently, here you'll find support for all sorts of piracy; however, in caution of their extremism Know Piracy is all talk. There's no ship, no cannons, and certainly no IP-infringement.

Furthermore, Know Piracy is in the fortunate position of mocking a position that is both far reaching and absurd. As such the site's own extremism can take a stark and obvious contrast to genuine suggestions for progress. Alternatively, if one can't or chooses not to distinguish this contrast, we welcome the advent of more piratey culture. Arg.


What's a Pirate?

Technically, a pirate is one who commits piracy: Criminal, non-wartime robbery at sea. They're are usually distinguished from smugglers and mercenaries; however, they can be closely tied in business. Beyond technicality, pirates have been romanticized into their own loosely defined theme. Key components of the theme include: freedom, greed, and adventure. The Culture tab addresses the theme in depth.

In terms of Intellectual-Property, Pirates are still those who commit piracy; however, piracy gets redefined as IP-infringement. The argument goes that IP-infringement is theft, piracy is theft, and therefore IP-infringement is piracy. Not entirely sure what happened to the sea there, but we'll let it slide. The more persistent question seems to be: Is IP-infringement theft?


Why care?

Often people forget that there are an exceptional number of reasons not to care. It's neither inconceivable nor deplorable to dismiss the discussion out-of-hand. After all, encouraging an entire thematic bias for a society is a bit extreme. No matter how cool pirates are.

The apathy can stand in the intellectual property argument as well because the system shouldn't affect anyone. It currently benefits some, punishes some, and irritates the hell out the others; but there are still people who can get away with ignoring it all together. That said, if it is in the way of some of your ambitions, you have a clear prerogative to reform the system. Intellectual property is a significant part of the current system, and it may be a part you need to reform.
